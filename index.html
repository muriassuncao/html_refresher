A Little History


Call it another gift from the gays to the straight universe. House Music had its humble beginning in 1977 at The Warehouse, a gay club in Chicago, patronized mainly by the black and latino community. The Warehouse's D.J. Frankie Knuckles, universally considered the Godfather of House experimented with Disco, a new European electronic sound, drums, and soul to create a "rawer" and "deeper" sound that would shape generations to come.

Some of today's subgenres of the original Chicago House include:
  • Deep House
  • Tech House
  • Tribal House
  • Electro House (well, maybe you can ship this one)
  • Funky House
  • Gospel House
  • UK Garage
  • Trance (skippable, as well)
  • Jackin' House
  • Soulful house
